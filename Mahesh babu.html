Biography

Mahesh Babu, born on August 9, 1975, in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, is a celebrated Indian film actor and producer primarily known for his work in Telugu cinema. He hails from a prominent film family; his father, Krishna, is a renowned actor and filmmaker, while his mother, Indira Devi, is a homemaker. Mahesh has an elder sister, Padmavathi, and a younger brother, Ramesh Babu, who is also involved in the film industry. Mahesh Babu was raised in a film-oriented environment, which sparked his interest in acting from a young age. He made his acting debut as a child artist in the 1989 film "Needa." He continued to act in several films during his childhood. He later pursued his education in commerce at St. Mary's College, Hyderabad. Mahesh Babu's career took off when he starred as a lead actor in the film "Rajakumarudu" (1999), which became a commercial success. He gained significant recognition with films like "Murari" (2001), "Okkadu" (2003), and "Pokiri" (2006), which established him as a leading star in Telugu cinema. His roles often portray strong, principled characters, resonating with audiences. Mahesh has consistently delivered hit films, including "Dookudu" (2011), "Baadshah" (2013), "Srimanthudu" (2015), and "Bharat Ane Nenu" (2018). He has received numerous accolades, including several Nandi Awards and Filmfare Awards. Mahesh Babu married actress Namrata Shirodkar in 2005. The couple has two children, a son named Gautam and a daughter named Sitara. Mahesh is known for his down-to-earth personality and often keeps his personal life private. Beyond acting, Mahesh Babu is actively involved in philanthropic activities, particularly in education and health. He founded the Heal-a-Child foundation, which supports children in need of medical treatment. He also owns the production company G. Mahesh Babu Entertainment Pvt. Ltd. Mahesh Babu is often referred to as the "Prince of Tollywood" due to his immense popularity and influence in the film industry. He has a vast fan base and is known for his stylish persona, making him one of the highest-paid actors in Indian cinema. His work has significantly contributed to the growth of Telugu cinema on national and international platforms. In summary, Mahesh Babu's journey from a child artist to a superstar exemplifies his talent and dedication to the film industry, solidifying his status as an iconic figure in Indian cinema.
